# Panorama Evaluation Checklist

Use this checklist after generating a panorama. The goal is not perfection, but a practical review of whether the image works as a believable 360-degree VR-style environment.

## Before Generation

- A reference image is attached, visible, or otherwise available.
- The prompt is used with the reference image, not by itself.
- The intended output is a 2:1 panorama.
- The scene should expand from the reference image instead of inventing an unrelated world.
- Test the result in a panorama or VR viewer, not only in a normal image viewer.

## Projection and Aspect Ratio

- The image uses a 2:1 aspect ratio, such as 2048 x 1024 or 4096 x 2048.
- The result looks like an equirectangular panorama, not a normal wide-angle photo.
- The scene contains information across the full horizontal span.
- The top and bottom areas represent sky, ceiling, ground, floor, or other plausible vertical coverage.

## Immersion and Spatial Coverage

- The viewer feels placed inside the environment, not outside looking at a flat scene.
- The panorama includes front, back, left, right, up, and down coverage.
- The space has believable foreground, midground, and background depth.
- The scene feels physically continuous and walkable where appropriate.
- Surrounding details support the reference image instead of distracting from it.

## Seam Quality

- The left and right edges appear to connect naturally.
- There is no obvious vertical border, lighting jump, or color mismatch.
- Objects do not abruptly cut off at the left-right seam.
- Important people, faces, text, signs, or major detailed objects are not placed on the seam.
- Repeated patterns near the seam do not look copied or broken.

## Horizon and Viewpoint

- The horizon is level and stable.
- The viewpoint feels close to natural human eye height.
- The image does not look tilted, warped, or like it was captured with a fisheye lens.
- The viewer position feels near the center of the scene.
- Ground, floor, sky, or ceiling areas support the sense of a full environment.

## Reference Image Consistency

- The generated scene preserves the reference image's mood and visual style.
- Lighting direction and color temperature feel consistent with the reference.
- Materials, textures, weather, era, and setting match the reference.
- Added details feel like logical extensions of the original image.
- The result does not introduce random objects that clash with the source.

## Common Failure Signs

- The image is just a stretched or widened version of the reference.
- The output looks like a poster, banner, or ordinary landscape photo.
- The left and right edges do not wrap cleanly.
- The horizon bends, waves, or shifts height unexpectedly.
- People, hands, faces, text, or complex objects are distorted.
- The top or bottom area collapses into confusing shapes.
- The scene has no clear back side or surrounding environment.
- The image contains watermarks, captions, labels, UI elements, or camera equipment.
